Why Do Autumn Leaves Change Color?

Every fall we enjoy the colors of the autumn leaves. As the days get shorter, trees are triggered into getting ready for winter. Trees use sunlight to turn water and carbon dioxide into oxygen and glucose, a process called photosynthesis. Glucose is a type of sugar that plants use as food for energy to grow. But during winter, there is not enough light or water for photosynthesis to take place. Trees produce less and less chlorophyll, until eventually they are not producing any. When this happens, carotenoids, which are already present in leaves, begin to show in yellows and orange colors. Red and purple leaf colors are produced from anthocyanins. Anthrocyanins result from a chemical reaction that allows trees to recover nutrients in the leaves before the leaves fall off the tree.
